froogalr

Find cheap eats in NYC for under $15.

Froogalr maps restaurants by zipcode with a price range of under $15 using data from the Factual Restaurants API. Users of froogalr can add favorite restaurants and post reviews to determine whether the restaurant was indeed worth the cheap price-point it totes.

deeds

A collaborative economy made easy.

Deeds, the first and simplest project I ever made using Rails, makes it easy for local communities to self-organize and encourages users to help one another by completing good deeds, or everyday tasks that could use a helping hand. Deeds takes the approach of other collaborative economies, hoping that users will share their abundance of physical assets and resources.

When as user creates a deed, their post can be viewed by the Deeds community. Other members may make proposals to those deeds and, once completed, the option to send a bonus payment to the helper becomes available using Venmo. While payment is not required, Deeds maintains that hard work should be compensated.

Oh SNAP!

A “simulator data toy” to see how Food Stamps affect families and children in America.

Oh SNAP allows users to see the amount of SNAP benefits they would have had before the cut and after, which went into effect in 2014, and what the process of shopping for food is like.

The technologies used to create this project include: Ruby, Rails, PostgreSQL, Backbone.js, Handlebars, FactoryGirl, RSPEC, Capybara, Simplecov, Devise, CanCanCan, Bootstrap, and Animate.css.
